Sticky Buns

Description

This sweet dessert is Pennsylvania Dutch class, and also one of my favorite breakfast orders at our local caf√© when paired with a good cup of coffee.

This is a family recipe that is best eaten warm from the oven. With this recipe, you can enjoy sleeping in and still have a fabulous breakfast, as these sticky buns can be prepared ahead, and kept in the fridge unbaked until the next morning.

Ingredients

  • 10 cups flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 2 cups warm milk
  • 4 eggs
  • 11 cup crisco
  • 2 packs (1 1/2 tablespoons) yeast

      • Syrup

    • 8 cups brown sugar
    • 1 cup molasses
    • 2 lbs. butter
    • 5 cups pecans, optional
    • 1 1/2 cups water

Steps

  1. Dissolve yeast in 1 cup of warm water, and let stand for 10 minutes
  2. Combine dry ingredients, then mix in yeast and eggs
  3. Stir in cirso and remaining liquid. Then add more flour as needed (1-2 cups) until dough is stiff enough to handle
  4. Knead, then let rise 2-2 1/2 hours. Knead a second time and let rise again
  5. Roll dough in oblong shape and sprinkle with cinnamon. Roll up and slice in 1/2 inch slices
  6. In a large saucepan, melt all syrup ingredients. Grease seven 9"x13" cake pans, and pour syrup into pans. Sprinkle pecans over syrup
  7. Place dough slices into pan on top of pecans, then let rise 30 minutes if baking immediately. To bake the next day, place rolls into fridge without rising. The next day, get rolls out of fridge 2 hours before baking.
  8. Bake at 350 degrees for 20 minutes. Allow to cool for 10 minutes, then turn out upside-down onto baking sheet.
